This study makes use of national register data to investigate the trends of health inequity in the total population of the Nordic countries from three time periods: pre-pandemic (2017-2019), pandemic (2020-2022) and post-pandemic (2023-2024) years. In doing so, we make use of aggregated Nordic register data, not only to investigate the unequal burden of covid-19 in these countries, but also to decompose changed patterns in social inequalities with regard to cause-specific mortality.
